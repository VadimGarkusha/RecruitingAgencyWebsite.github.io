Web Designer/Developer

Faculty / Division: University of Toronto Scarborogh
Department: UTSC: Information and Instructional Technology Services
Campus: Scarborogh

Description

Information & Instructional Technology Services (IITS) provides the technological vision and leadership for the development and implementation of information systems, processes, and associated technology to support the University of Toronto Scarborough Campus' academic mission and administrative operation. IITS works closely with faculty, staff, and student groups to provide timely, top-quality service and technological solutions across the University of Toronto Scarborough community.

Under the general supervision of the Web Designer Team Lead, the incumbent is responsible for the design and development of Web sites and Web-based applications at UTSC. Duties include creating and editing graphics, Web pages, and templates for the use of academic departments and administrative offices; laying out contents and graphics; creating training documentation; meeting with clients to understand their objectives and goals; advising necessary or recommended enhancements; collaborating with Web server and database administrators to design technical components of Web sites and Web applications; other duties as assigned.

Qualifications:
(Minimum Qualifications)

Education:

University degree in a related area of study or an equivalent combination of education and experience.

Experience:

Demonstrated ability to create, produce and implement website designs. Ability to create or edit digital design assets, including images and other content. Ability to manage websites on a continued basis. Experience in administering and developing a Drupal content management systems for a multi-contributor environment, using dynamic database-driven websites and applications, and using social media and new media tools – including social networking sites, video sharing sites, blogs, etc. Demonstrated web design skills, including layout, typography, graphic design principles and colour theory, is required. Clear understanding of how design translates to code. Working knowledge of current web design and development technologies is required. Ability to produce clean and well-documented code for all web projects. Working knowledge of HTML, and CSS. Working knowledge of Illustrator, Photoshop, inDesign, Image Ready, Dreamweaver, Fireworks. Familiarity with Web programming languages, Javascript, and PHP. Knowledge of Microsoft applications and Adobe Acrobat on both Mac and Windows operating systems. Understanding of client-server, web, and database technologies. Ability to create and interpret mindmap documents.
